About Me

What are we if nothing but the sum of our experiences? I am always willing to try the new, be uncomfortable, take a chance. My experiences range from working on a sport fishing boat in Alaska to owning a private tutoring business in South Korea.

Attending the Uniersity of Orgeon and not knowing what to study, I thought business administration was proabably a reasonable field. I majored in Finance, but in my third year, my artistic itch had to be scratched. I took 2 quarters of art

classes to re-evaluate my current path. It was fun, but ultimately decided that a business degree would serve me better than one in art - the jury is still out on that one.After gratduating from the University of Oregon in '94', I immediately eschewed the idea of a "real" job and landed a contract bartending on a cruiseship. Always looking for the next challenge, I then moved on to restaurant managment and then to merchant processing.

One of the most pivotal moments in my life had its genesis via an advertisement to teach ESL in South Korea. I interviewed and three weeks later I landed at Gimpo Int'l Airport. I spent the next twenty-one years building a successful life and small education business. During that time the path from ESL instuctor, to acdademic director, to starting my own business was fairly organic.

For the last 2 years I had been considereing making a change. I decided that this point in my life would be as good as any to try something completely new; to reinvent myself, so we loaded up the truck and we moved to... The USA.

Planning to do things I just didn't have time to do while running a business, the first step in this life evolution life has taken me down a road nevertravelled by me before; coding! Where it takes me, no body knows. So far this life transistion has been exciting and very uncomfortable; just what it should be if I am to grow from it, right?
